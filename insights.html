Structural Monitoring of the Old Bridge

expand_more

This case study details the deployment of fiber-optic sensors and AI-driven anomaly detection to monitor the structural integrity of a 150-year-old historic bridge. The system successfully identified micro-fractures caused by increased traffic load, allowing for timely reinforcement and preventing catastrophic failure. Key results include a 40% reduction in manual inspection costs and a 99.9% computational confidence in predictive stress models.

Predictive Maintenance on Metro Line 3 Viaduct

expand_more

Utilizing vibration and acoustic sensors, our AI model analyzed the health of the Metro Line 3 viaduct. The system predicted a bearing failure with 3 weeks of lead time, enabling a planned maintenance window that avoided service disruption. This proactive approach improved system reliability and saved an estimated $1.2M in potential emergency repair costs and associated economic impact.

Climate Control for the National Archives

expand_more

This project involved implementing an intelligent climate control system for the National Archives building. By analyzing environmental data and building occupancy patterns, the AI optimizes HVAC operations to maintain strict temperature and humidity levels for artifact preservation while reducing energy consumption by 22%, significantly improving the facility's sustainability index.
